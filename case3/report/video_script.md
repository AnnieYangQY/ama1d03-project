# Case III Video Presentation Script (≤10 minutes)

## Slide 1 — Introduction (1 min)

- Course: AMA1D03, Section II Case III
- Topic: Defined Contribution Plan and MPF in Hong Kong
- Case: Peter (age 20) vs Mary (age 40), both retire at 65
- Question: Is mandatory MPF enough? Should they add extra contributions?

## Slide 2 — MPF / DC System Overview (2 min)

- DC plan: benefit depends on contributions + investment returns
- MPF since Dec 2000: 5% employee + 5% employer
- Fund types: Global, HK, mixed, bond, etc.
- TVC: tax-deductible voluntary contributions up to HK$60,000/year
- Hong Kong three pillars: OALA (1st), MPF (2nd), personal savings (3rd)

## Slide 3 — Modelling Assumptions (1 min)

- Start: Dec 2000, salary HK$20,000, monthly MC HK$2,000
- Salary grows with HK CPI
- Peter → Global fund (ACWI); Mary → HK fund (2800.HK)
- Retirement income: 4% withdrawal rule
- Show assumptions table briefly

## Slide 4 — Key Results: Peter vs Mary (3 min)

Show `fig1_balance_over_age.png` and baseline table:

| | Peter | Mary |
|---|-------|------|
| Years | 45 | 25 |
| Balance | HK$4.86M | HK$1.03M |
| Replacement ratio | 35% | 11% |

- Peter's investment gains (HK$3.4M) exceed total contributions
- Mary ends with only 11% replacement — far below 60% target
- Show `fig3_start_age_sensitivity.png`: start age matters more than fund choice

## Slide 5 — TVC and Social Security (2 min)

Show `fig4_tvc_marginal_effect.png` and `fig2_income_breakdown.png`:

- Peter needs ~HK$1,700/month extra for 60% RR
- Mary needs ~HK$10,500/month extra for 60% RR
- OALA (HK$2,065–4,185/month) helps Mary but does not close the gap
- Peter likely ineligible for OALA due to asset test

## Slide 6 — Conclusions (1 min)

1. Mandatory MPF alone is insufficient for both
2. Early start (Peter) is the strongest advantage in DC systems
3. Mary must combine TVC, OALA, and third-pillar savings
4. Policy implication: Hong Kong's DC model places retirement risk on individuals
